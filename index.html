Core Principles of Blackbody Radiation

Wien’s Displacement Law

This law explains why the color of a glowing object changes with temperature. It states that the peak wavelength (λmax) of emitted radiation is inversely proportional to the temperature (T). Hotter objects peak at shorter wavelengths, shifting from red to blue to ultraviolet.

λmax = b / T

Stefan-Boltzmann Law

This law describes why hotter objects are so much brighter. The total power (P) radiated by a blackbody is proportional to the fourth power of its temperature (T). A small increase in temperature leads to a massive increase in radiated energy.

P = σT4

Conclusion of the Simulation

This interactive simulation demonstrates that the spectrum of radiation emitted by a blackbody is strongly dependent on its temperature, providing a clear visual confirmation of key physics principles.

  • ✓ Temperature Dictates the Spectrum: Increasing temperature causes the peak emission to shift toward shorter, higher-energy wavelengths (from infrared to visible to ultraviolet) and dramatically increases the total radiated intensity.
  • ✓ Laws Confirmed: The observed shifts in peak wavelength and intensity directly validate Wien’s Displacement Law and the Stefan-Boltzmann Law, respectively.
  • ✓ Explains Real-World Phenomena: This relationship explains why cool objects like Earth radiate invisible heat, why the Sun's light peaks in the visible spectrum that supports life, and why very hot stars like Sirius A appear blue.
